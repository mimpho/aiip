Pairing rationale
A transitional serif for the human voice, a humanist grotesque for clarity.
Spectral carries the emotional weight in headlines — it's a screen-native serif, warm without being decorative. It directly continues the serif-H1 the immunologist approved in the prototype.
Hanken Grotesk handles all body and UI: friendly, open, highly legible at small sizes for anxious parents and dense professional reading alike.
JetBrains Mono earns the technical register — source citations, reference IDs (PMID, Orphanet #), and the Professional UI labels.

Family ↔ Professional · differentiation options
A · Shared baseRECOMMENDED
One identical dark base. Only the accent changes. Cleanest, most maintainable — solves the “too similar” problem by not pretending they're different.
B · Tinted bases
A 4–6% accent tint warms each base (blue-leaning vs green-leaning). Subtle separation while staying one family. Pushes the prototype further apart.
C · Mood split
Family lifts to a softer, lighter surface; Professional sinks darker and denser. Strongest emotional contrast, but two bases to maintain.
