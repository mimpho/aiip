My child has a fever
When in doubt, always contact your medical team.
AIIP
In children with a primary immunodeficiency, a fever can be an important warning sign. According to IPOPI guidance, any fever above 38 °C (100.4 °F) should be assessed by your medical team as soon as possible. If your specialist isn't reachable, go to the emergency department and mention the diagnosis.
Source: IPOPI — Family Guide for PID

Bruton agammaglobulinemia — protocol
AIIP · SCIENTIFIC AGENT
ORPHANET ESID PUBMED
X-linked agammaglobulinemia (XLA) is caused by mutations in the BTK gene (Xq21.3–q22), with near-complete absence of mature B lymphocytes and all immunoglobulin classes (Orphanet #47132). Treatment is lifelong immunoglobulin replacement (IVIG/SCIG), targeting trough IgG >8 g/L per ESID guidelines.
